# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Applicants & nominees** — tech founders, professionals, students, enterprises, and government bodies submitting for one of 16 awards across 5 category tracks (A–E). They need clarity on eligibility, deadlines, and what winning means.

**ICT industry insiders** — Nepal's tech sector follows the awards closely. They track who won, scan the category landscape, and use the site as a barometer of the ecosystem's progress since 2016.

**General public & media** — Curious Nepalis, press, and students who want to understand what the ICT Awards is, who the past winners are, and why it matters. First impressions and storytelling are critical here.

**Sponsors & partners** — Organizations evaluating brand association. They assess credibility, reach, and alignment. The awards' 10-year track record and institutional backing (ICTC Nepal) are the pitch.

## Product Purpose

The ICT Awards Nepal is the country's premier annual technology recognition event, now in its 11th edition (Grand Finale: December 18, 2026), organized by ICTC Nepal. The website is the awards' primary public face: it drives applications, informs the public, showcases a decade of Nepali tech excellence, and builds the ecosystem's collective identity. Success looks like a site where any Nepali — a 21-year-old student building their first startup, or a government minister evaluating digital governance — immediately understands the weight and warmth of this recognition.

## Brand Personality

**Aspirational, Inclusive, Proud.**

The ICT Awards isn't for a select elite — it spans startups, students, women leaders, diaspora, province-based founders, enterprises, and government bodies. The tone is warm but confident: we celebrate what Nepal's tech sector has become and what it is becoming. Not ceremonial for its own sake. Not flashy. Pride without pomposity.

## Anti-references

- **Generic award-site templates** — trophies as hero images, stock photos of handshakes and certificates, marble textures, serif-heavy "prestige" styling. These feel frozen in 2010 and misrepresent Nepal's forward-looking tech scene.
- **Blue-gradient dev conference sites** — the "ICT/tech event" reflex palette of cobalt blue, generic tech iconography, and dense schedule grids. This is a celebration, not a summit.
- **Overly corporate award sites** — think Fortune 500 award programs with grey palettes and formal register. The ICT Awards is sector-led and community-rooted; it should feel alive, not institutional.

## Design Principles

1. **Nepal's tech story, not a generic award event.** The 10-year winner archive, the 5-track category system, the Dec 18 countdown — these are specific to this ecosystem. Lean into what makes Nepal's ICT journey distinctive.

2. **Aspiration over ceremony.** The hero isn't a trophy — it's an invitation. Every surface should make an applicant feel "I could be up there" more than "I'm watching from the outside."

3. **Breadth without confusion.** Five very different audience groups (Group A–E) need clear, non-competing pathways. The category color system (indigo→red spectrum) exists for exactly this — use it to orient, not decorate.

4. **The archive is the brand.** Ten years of winners is the credibility argument. It should be discoverable and celebrated, not buried as a footnote.

5. **Institutional authority through confidence, not decoration.** ICTC Nepal's backing means the design earns trust through clarity and consistency — not through heavy ornamentation or formal register.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Special attention to:
- Color contrast: the gold (#F7B413) and spectrum palette must always be tested against their backgrounds. Group E red (#C20F1A, not #E0121E) used for small text.
- Reduced motion: countdown and scroll animations must have `prefers-reduced-motion` fallbacks.
- Keyboard navigation: apply/category browsing flows must be fully operable by keyboard.
- The "Woman Icon" and "Rising Star" categories signal inclusion as a core value — the site's accessibility posture should match.
