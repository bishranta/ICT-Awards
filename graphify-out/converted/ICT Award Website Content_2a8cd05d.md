Introduction
WHAT IS THE EVENT ABOUT?
The ICT Award is a prestigious award in Nepal, which is given to individuals, innovation and organizations for outstanding achievements and excellence in the field of Information and Communications Technology (ICT).

This could include innovative use of technology, exceptional contributions to the ICT industry, or outstanding products or services in the ICT sector of Nepal. The award is often given by industry organizations, governmental agencies and education institutions that are focused on promoting innovation, technology, and excellence in the field of ICT.

WHY THIS EVENT?
The ICT Award accelerates progress on the SDGs based on the “four I’s” framework based on building Infrastructure, securing Investment, promoting Innovation, and ensuring Inclusivity. ICT forms the backbone of today’s digital economy and has enormous potential to fast forward progress on the SDGs and improve people’s lives in fundamental ways. We are enthusiastic about the chance to strengthen Nepal’s digital industry by setting the standard for awards and recognition in the technology sector of Nepal.

Significance of the ICT Award
The ICT Award is widely regarded as Nepal’s most prestigious recognition in the field of information and communication technology. It contributes to shaping the nation’s digital future through the following key dimensions:
Fostering Startup Culture and Innovation: By identifying and honoring early-stage ventures and tech-based innovations, the award stimulates a culture of entrepreneurship and experimentation within the tech community.
Celebrating Digital Impact: The award highlights groundbreaking digital campaigns, impactful ICT solutions, and technological interventions that are improving lives and transforming systems across the country.
Inspiring the Next Generation: Each edition showcases role models—from visionary entrepreneurs to young innovators—encouraging the youth to pursue careers in science, technology, and entrepreneurship.
Enabling Collaboration and Growth: Nominees and winners gain access to an ecosystem of peers, investors, mentors, and policymakers, enabling growth through collaboration and cross-sector partnerships.
Raising National and Global Visibility: Recognition at the ICT Award often translates into increased credibility, media exposure, and international interest—helping projects scale and enter new markets.
Setting Standards for Excellence: Through a rigorous selection process and defined evaluation metrics, the award promotes accountability, quality, and ethical digital practices across the sector.
Contributing to Economic and Social Development: By elevating technology-led solutions, the ICT Award supports Nepal’s broader development goals, from digital inclusion and financial access to education, health, and governance.
Empowering Local Communities: Many recognized projects are designed to address local challenges—ranging from access to services in remote areas to improving education delivery—amplifying social impact at the grassroots level.
Shaping the National Agenda: With strong backing from government bodies, academia, and industry, the award influences digital policy and supports the creation of a robust ICT ecosystem.
Celebrating Diversity in Tech: The award spans multiple domains including e-governance, fintech, edtech, agritech, healthtech, and more—highlighting the wide-ranging potential of technology to drive change.

As we commemorate a decade of digital excellence, the 11th edition of the ICT Award is more than just an award, it's a celebration of Nepal’s progress toward becoming a digitally empowered nation. It reflects how far we've come, while igniting the vision for the future: a Nepal where technology is inclusive, empowering, and transformative.

Selection Procedure
The selection process for the ICT Award is designed to ensure transparency, credibility, and fairness. Each award category has a structured evaluation methodology, tailored to the nature of the recognition being granted. While the specific criteria may vary by category, the core factors typically include:
Impact on the ICT industry
Level of innovation and originality
Quality and execution of work
Creativity and societal relevance
### Category A
(Startup ICT Award 2025, Product ICT Award 2025, Rising Star Innovation ICT Award 2025, Social Innovation ICT Award 2025)
Selection Process:
Call for Nominations
Preliminary Criteria Check & Document Review
First Phase of Jury Evaluation
Selection of Top 12/15 Finalists
Public Idea/Startup Showcase
Presentation and Speed Dating Session with Grand Jury
Television Broadcast Featuring Top 12 Finalists
Top 5 Finalist Selection
Public Voting Among Top 5
Field/Industry Visit for Final Evaluation
Grand Finale and Winner Announcement
### Category B
(Pioneer ICT Award 2025, Professional Excellence ICT Award 2025, Woman Icon ICT Award 2025, Entrepreneur ICT Award 2025, Nepali Diaspora ICT Award 2025)
Selection Process:
Call for Nominations
Preliminary Criteria Check & Document Review
First Phase of Jury Evaluation
Second Phase Evaluation – Top 10 Selection
Third Phase Evaluation Internal Presentations of Selected Nominees
Final Selection of Top 3 Finalists (Internal Voting)
Grand Finale and Winner Announcement
The jury and advisory team conduct three to four rounds of evaluations, incorporating internal feedback. Field visits to offices or organizations may be conducted during the final phase to assess nominees in real-world settings.
### Category C
(Digital Governance ICT Award 2025, Digital Education ICT Award 2025 – Public Sector, Digital Education ICT Award 2025– Private Sector, Digital Services ICT Award 2025- Enterprise Sector, Digital Services ICT Award 2025- Neo Sector)
Selection Process:
Call for Nominations
Preliminary Criteria Check & Document Review
First Phase of Jury Evaluation
Second Phase – Top 10 Selection
Third Phase – Presentations of Selected Nominees (Internal)
Final Selection of Top 3 Finalists (Internal Voting)
Grand Finale and Winner Announcement
Over 25 jury meetings are conducted throughout the evaluation period to ensure comprehensive and impartial decision-making.
The jury panel consists of industry experts, academicians, government representatives, and ICT professionals.
Field assessments and site visits are integral to the evaluation process, particularly for startup, innovation, governance, and service-related categories.

FAQs
1. What are the eligibility criteria for applying for the Startup ICT Award 2026?
Ans: The Startup ICT Award 2026 is open to Nepali tech-startups incorporated within the last five years, specifically from F.Y. 2078/079 onwards. Eligible startups should have demonstrated substantial institutional growth, successfully scaled their products and services, and diversified their business models. They must showcase resilience and innovation within the ICT sector, highlighting their potential impact and promising prospects in Nepal.

2. What are the eligibility criteria for applying for the Product ICT Award 2026?
Ans: The Product ICT Award 2026 recognizes innovative tech products from Nepali companies or organizations. Eligible products include software, hardware, or a mix of both, launched between 14th April 2025 – 15th July 2026 (2082 Baishakh 1 – 2083 Ashar 31). These products should demonstrate how ICT positively impacts businesses or community life, aiming to showcase innovation and practical benefits to users.

3. What are the eligibility criteria for applying for the Social Innovation ICT Award 2026?
Ans: The Social Innovation ICT Award 2026 celebrates innovative projects from academia, government, and NGOs that use Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to drive positive social change. Eligible initiatives should demonstrate creativity and have a measurable or potential impact in areas like education, public health, rights protection, connectivity, and inclusion. Projects must have started within the last five years, showcasing sustainability and a commitment to improving societal well-being through ICT solutions.

4. Can applicants from last year also apply for this year's ICT Award?
Ans: Previous year applicants are welcome to apply for this year’s ICT Award.

5. Is the form editable after submission?
Ans: The form can be editable till the date of deadline.

6. Are there any charges for applying?
Ans: There are no charges for applying to any award category. Applicants can freely apply to any category they believe they qualify for.

7. Can Nepali startups apply in the South Asia Startup ICT Award 2026?
Ans: Nepali startups are indeed eligible and qualified to apply for the South Asia Startup ICT Award 2026. They are encouraged to showcase their innovations and contributions within the regional startup ecosystem through this category.

Categories
Category-A
Startup ICT AWARD 2025
This award recognizes a Nepali tech startup that has effectively leveraged Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to drive innovation, growth, and impact in Nepal’s ICT sector. The startup should demonstrate strong institutional development, scalable products or services, a diversified business model, and resilience in the country’s evolving digital landscape.

Eligibility: Nepali startups incorporated on or after fiscal year 2077/078 — that is, between 2077 Shrawan 1, and 2082 Asar 32 (July 16, 2020 to July 16, 2025).

Startup ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Crimson Tech Pvt. Ltd.

Product ICT AWARD 2025
This award honors a groundbreaking ICT product—whether software, hardware, or a combination of both—that has made a significant impact on business operations or community life. It recognizes innovation that clearly demonstrates how ICT can transform the way businesses operate or improve people’s lives. The product should reflect originality, practical application, and measurable benefits within Nepal’s ICT landscape.

Eligibility: Products released between April 14, 2024 (2081 Baishakh 1), and July 15, 2025 (2082 Ashar 32) by any Nepali company or organization.

Product ICT Award 2024 was awarded to APIMS (API Management System).

Rising Star Innovation ICT AWARD 2025
This award recognizes outstanding contributions and innovations in Nepal’s ICT sector by young individuals or students. It highlights innovative ideas, academic research projects, or independent technological solutions that demonstrate creativity, impact, and potential for further development. The award celebrates the spirit of innovation among youth and encourages the next generation of tech leaders to bring forward meaningful change through ICT. Projects submitted should be original and must not be affiliated with the R&D efforts of any established company.

Eligibility: Open to full-time students or individuals below 30 years of age (born on or after Shrawan 1, 2052 B.S. / July 17, 1995 A.D.). All team members must meet this age criterion.

Rising Star Innovation ICT Award 2024 was awarded to SpeakUp Nepal.

Social Innovation ICT AWARD 2025
This award honors socially impactful, technology-driven initiatives that leverage ICT to address critical challenges in areas such as health, education, agriculture, tourism, climate change, human rights, and social inclusion. Open to academic institutions, government bodies, development agencies, non-profits, or private organizations, the initiative must demonstrate innovation, measurable impact, and alignment with broader public goals. Projects should empower communities, enhance service delivery, and promote sustainable development through inclusive digital solutions. Private sector-led initiatives must be implemented in collaboration with government, non-profits, or development partners to ensure relevance to community needs.

Eligibility: The submitted product, project, or initiative must have been initiated between Shrawan 1, 2077 (July 16, 2020) and Chaitra 31, 2081 (April 13, 2025), and must have been actively running for a minimum of 3 months as of July 16, 2025

Social Innovation ICT Award 2024 was awarded to GeoKrishi.

Category-B
Pioneer ICT AWARD 2025
This award honors a senior individual who has made significant and lasting contributions to the promotion and development of Nepal’s ICT sector. Often working behind the scenes, these pioneers have played a crucial role in shaping the industry and expanding Nepal’s technology and services both nationally and internationally over the past 20 years or more. Their dedication and impact have been unprecedented and widely recognized within the ICT community. Nominations can be submitted by the individual themselves or by others wishing to acknowledge their contributions.

Eligibility: Veteran individuals who have contributed to the development and growth of Nepal’s ICT sector for at least 20 years.

Pioneer ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Prof. Dr. Suresh Raj Sharma, Founding Vice Chancellor, Kathmandu University (KU).

Professional Excellence ICT AWARD 2025
This award recognizes and honors ICT professionals who have demonstrated exceptional expertise and excellence in their specified ICT-focused domain—whether in academia, industry, corporate sectors, or technical communities. Eligible professionals should have a proven track record of outstanding performance, innovation, and leadership that distinguishes them as experts in their ICT field. They must have made significant contributions that have advanced knowledge, practices, or technologies within their ICT domain, and positively impacted their organizations, communities, or society at large. While their work may have broad relevance, nominees are expected to be actively engaged in Nepal’s ICT ecosystem, contributing meaningfully to the country’s digital growth and development. By consistently exhibiting professional excellence, these individuals play a key role in driving Nepal’s ICT progress, inspiring others, and elevating standards within the sector.

Eligibility: ICT professionals with a minimum of seven years of experience working in Nepal, including at least five years of continuous experience in the same domain, who have played a key role in driving innovation and excellence.

Professional Excellence ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Dr. Bishesh Khanal, Co-Founder, Director & Research Scientist, NAAMII.

Entrepreneur ICT AWARD 2025
This award celebrates visionary ICT entrepreneurs who have demonstrated leadership in driving innovation, creating jobs, and contributing significantly to Nepal’s economic development. It honors individuals whose entrepreneurial journeys have inspired a generation and helped shape the ICT landscape both within Nepal and internationally. Their achievements reflect resilience, strategic vision, and a lasting impact on the growth of the digital economy.

Eligibility: Entrepreneurs with more than 10 years of experience in the ICT sector, with a proven track record of success, innovation, and positive impact. The nomination can be submitted by the entrepreneur themselves or by any third party.

Entrepreneur ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Mr. Manohar Adhikari, Managing Director, Foodmandu.
Woman Icon ICT AWARD 2025
This award honors a distinguished female leader who has made significant contributions to Nepal’s ICT sector for Fifteen years or more. It recognizes and celebrates the achievements and leadership of women who have played a vital role in advancing the tech industry in Nepal. Through their vision, resilience, and dedication, these women have not only driven innovation and growth but also paved the way for future generations of women in technology. Their leadership inspires others to overcome barriers, pursue careers in ICT, and contribute meaningfully to Nepal’s digital transformation. Nominations can be submitted by the nominee herself or by others who wish to acknowledge her impact and dedication.

Eligibility: Female leaders who have contributed to Nepal’s ICT sector for at least 12 years.

The Woman Icon ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Ms. Bandana Sharma, Senior Manager(IT) Nepal Bank Limited.

Nepali Diaspora ICT AWARD 2025
This award honors an individual of Nepali origin residing abroad who has achieved notable success in the international ICT arena. Through their outstanding contributions, leadership, or innovation in the global tech industry, the awardee not only exemplifies excellence but also helps position Nepal as a country with capable and globally competitive talent. Their achievements serve as an inspiration to the Nepali ICT community, positively impacting Nepali society by promoting knowledge sharing, technology transfer, and opportunities for collaboration. This recognition strengthens Nepal’s visibility and credibility in the global technology landscape, fostering greater avenues for investment and development.

Eligibility: Individuals of Nepali origin who are currently based abroad and have made significant contributions in the field of Information and Communication Technology ecosystem and community in Nepal and globally.

Nepali Diaspora ICT Award 2024 was awarded to Mrs. Nami Baral, Founder & CEO, Niural Inc.
Category-C
Digital Governance ICT AWARD 2025
This award recognizes government agencies that have demonstrated exceptional success in supporting, employing, and facilitating digital governance through innovative use of ICT. It honors local governing bodies (Palikas), ministries, departments, or their specialized ICT initiatives that have effectively incorporated and implemented digital tools to enhance governance and public service delivery.

Eligibility: Government organizations, including local bodies, ministries, departments, or their ICT initiatives, that have shown exemplary performance in using ICT to serve their constituencies and improve governance. Self-nominations are welcome.

The Digital Governance ICT Award 2024 was awarded to the Supreme Court of Nepal.

Digital Education ICT AWARD 2025 (Public Sector)
This award recognizes public educational institutions—schools and colleges—that have demonstrated outstanding excellence in integrating ICT as an effective tool for teaching and learning. It honors institutions that have successfully adopted digital technologies to enhance education quality, improve student engagement, and set a benchmark for digital education in Nepal’s public sector.

Eligibility: Public schools and colleges that have effectively implemented ICT in their teaching, learning, and pedagogy, demonstrating exemplary success in digital education. Nominations can be submitted by a representative of the institution or by any individual.

Digital Education ICT Award 2024 (Public Sector) was awarded to Sukuna Secondary School, Morang, Koshi Province.
Digital Education ICT AWARD 2025 (Private Sector)
This award celebrates private educational institutions—schools and colleges—that have demonstrated exemplary use of ICT to enhance educational delivery, pedagogy, and learning outcomes. It recognizes private institutions excelling in integrating digital technologies as effective tools for teaching, learning, and innovative pedagogical practices.

Eligibility: Private schools and colleges with proven success in applying ICT across teaching, learning, and pedagogy are eligible to nominate themselves. Nominations can be submitted by a representative of the institution or by any individual.

Digital Education ICT Award 2024 (Private Sector) was awarded to Learning Realm International (LRI) Secondary School, Kalanki, Kathmandu.
Digital Services ICT AWARD 2025 (Enterprise Sector)
This award recognizes the organizations and enterprises that have delivered exceptional services to the public. It recognizes how traditional organizations have transformed through digitalization, leveraging technology to enhance service delivery and improve public access. The focus is on the overall transformation of the organization and its services—not just on individual software or digital products. The award honors those that have made their services more accessible, efficient, and user-friendly by implementing innovative digital solutions to better serve the public.

Eligibility: Open to organizations that have digitally transformed their overall services to better serve the public as a whole—not just individual products or software. Examples include hospitals, insurance companies, banks, and similar institutions.

Digital Services ICT AWARD 2025 (Neo Sector)
This award recognizes private institutions, organizations, or their specialized ICT initiatives that have demonstrated outstanding use of technology to digitalize and enhance their services. It honors private sector companies across banking, insurance, health, agriculture, tourism, telecommunications, fintech, and other fields that have transformed traditional manual or physical services into accessible, efficient, and user-friendly digital solutions. Examples include ride-sharing platforms, neo banks, digital wallets, and similar innovations that provide tangible benefits to the public by improving service delivery through innovative digital technologies.

Eligibility:
Open to organizations or initiatives in Nepal that have developed innovative digital services replacing manual or physical processes. These solutions should clearly improve public access, convenience, and service quality by making previously manual services faster, easier, and more user-friendly. Examples include ride-sharing platforms, neo banks, digital wallets, and similar innovations

Digital Services ICT Award 2024 was awarded to IME Digital Solution.
Category-D – Regional Recognition
South Asia Startup ICT AWARD 2025
The South Asia Startup ICT Award 2025 recognizes impactful startups from across South Asia—including Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka—that have made notable contributions to the region’s growing tech and innovation ecosystem. As South Asia emerges as a dynamic market for digital transformation, this award celebrates startups that are leveraging ICT to solve pressing challenges, drive economic growth, and create scalable solutions with regional and global relevance. This category is part of the broader ICT Award 2025 and aims to foster cross-border collaboration, highlight entrepreneurial excellence, and showcase the region’s potential on the global tech stage.

Eligibility: ICT startups incorporated between January 2021 and December 2025 in any of the eight South Asian countries listed above.

The South Asia ICT Award 2024 was awarded to the Hyphen SCS (India).

Category-E – Recognitions
Province Startup ICT Recognition
This special recognition is dedicated to startups that are registered and operating within one of Nepal’s seven provinces. It celebrates emerging provincial startups that have demonstrated notable growth, innovation, and impact in their respective regions. The aim is to encourage balanced entrepreneurial development across the country by highlighting promising startups from outside the Kathmandu Valley.

Eligibility: Startups incorporated on or after Fiscal Year 2077/078 and operating within any of Nepal’s seven provinces.
